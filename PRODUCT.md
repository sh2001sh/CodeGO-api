# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Developers, API consumers, and site operators use this gateway to buy quota, manage API access, monitor usage, and configure upstream AI model routing. Most users arrive with a task-oriented intent: confirm balance, purchase a suitable package, join a group-buy room, copy an endpoint, or inspect consumption.

## Product Purpose

new-api provides a unified AI API gateway with authentication, billing, quota management, model availability, and administrative operations. The current product direction prioritizes a simpler purchase and growth system: wallet, packages, and group buying should be easy to find, easy to understand, and reliable during payment and quota settlement.

## Brand Personality

Practical, trustworthy, efficient. The interface should feel like an operations console for paid AI infrastructure: clear status, low ambiguity, and confident financial actions.

## Anti-references

Avoid game-like or ornamental surfaces for core billing flows, hidden purchase paths, decorative motion around payment, and marketing-heavy card grids that obscure prices, quota, validity, or risk. Do not remove or replace protected new-api or QuantumNous project identity.

## Design Principles

- Put financial decisions in direct, auditable flows with visible price, quota, validity, and payment state.
- Keep navigation flat for ordinary users; promote wallet, packages, group buying, keys, and usage over rarely used gamification features.
- Prefer existing product UI vocabulary over novelty; consistency matters more than decorative surprise.
- Make growth mechanics explicit but not coercive: group-buy bonuses and renewal rewards should be discoverable without making normal purchase feel penalized.
- Preserve admin capability and database compatibility while simplifying the ordinary user surface.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Maintain keyboard-reachable controls, visible focus states, readable contrast, localized copy through the existing i18n system where applicable, and reduced-motion-safe interactions. Payment and quota states must expose clear success, pending, and failure feedback.
