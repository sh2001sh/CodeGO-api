# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Mixed user base: both technical users (developers/admins managing API quotas and channels) and general end users (consumers seeking AI services through simple, fun experiences). Technical users interact during work hours for account management and quota operations. General users interact in casual contexts for service consumption and credit purchases.

## Product Purpose

CodexForAll is a next-generation LLM gateway and AI asset management system. It aggregates 40+ upstream AI providers (OpenAI, Claude, Gemini, Azure, AWS Bedrock, etc.) behind a unified API, with user management, billing, rate limiting, and an admin dashboard. The blind box feature specifically gamifies quota purchases to make AI credit acquisition engaging for non-technical users while maintaining professional reliability for technical operations.

## Brand Personality

Friendly and warm, efficient and direct. The interface balances approachability with task-focused clarity—users should feel welcomed but never slowed down. Avoid cold enterprise formality, but also avoid over-cuteness that undermines trust.

## Anti-references

- Cold, intimidating enterprise dashboards (Oracle, SAP) that feel exclusive to experts
- Over-gamified mobile games with excessive animations and exploitative dark patterns
- Generic SaaS templates with identical card grids and purple gradients
- Sterile white admin panels with no personality or visual warmth

## Design Principles

1. **Progressive disclosure**: Show technical users full details and advanced controls; show general users simplified flows with clear outcomes
2. **Trust through clarity**: Every transaction, quota change, and reward must be immediately transparent—no hidden costs or confusing states
3. **Warmth without compromise**: Friendly copy and visual warmth never sacrifice speed or information density for users who need efficiency
4. **Gamification with dignity**: Blind box mechanics should feel fun and exciting, not manipulative or childish

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- Target WCAG 2.1 AA compliance minimum
- Support reduced motion preferences (all animations must have reduced-motion fallbacks)
- Ensure 4.5:1 contrast for body text, 3:1 for large text
- Keyboard navigation for all interactive elements
- Clear focus indicators for all states
- Support color blindness (don't rely on color alone for information)
